# Literature Review: Similar Prior Studies on Anaesthetic Vaporizer Secondary Market Prices and Desflurane Regulation

## Summary

**No prior study was found that directly examines secondary market (online marketplace) price trends for anaesthetic vaporizers in response to environmental regulation.** The current study appears to be novel in its specific focus.

---

## Related Literature Identified

These papers address desflurane phase-out but from a clinical/environmental perspective — NOT secondary market economics:

|-----------|-------|----------------------|
| **Lehmann et al. | **Moonesinghe (2024)** Anaesthesia 79(3):237-241 | Editorial: "Desflurane decommissioning: more than meets the eye." Discusses broader implications of desflurane phase-out in UK/EU. | Policy discussion only; no market data. |

### 5. Regulation-Driven Asset Depreciation in Other Sectors

No peer-reviewed study was identified that quantitatively measures the impact of environmental regulation on secondary market prices for **any** category of medical equipment. The closest analogy comes from:
- **Automotive sector**: Diesel vehicle resale value decline following EU emissions regulations (numerous grey literature reports, but different asset class)
- **Energy sector**: Stranded asset literature examining fossil fuel equipment depreciation due to climate policy

## Novelty Assessment

| Dimension | Prior Literature | Our Study |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Data source** | Drug procurement databases, hospital purchasing records | eBay Terapeak (consumer-to-consumer secondary market) |
| **Outcome measure** | Drug consumption (mL), drug cost ($), CO₂eq emissions | Equipment resale transaction price ($) |
| **Analytical approach** | Before-and-after usage comparison, cost modeling | Time-series trend analysis (Spearman ρ, Kendall τ), interrupted pre/post comparison |
| **Control group** | Pre-intervention period only | Sevoflurane and isoflurane vaporizers as concurrent controls |
| **Research question** | "Does desflurane usage/cost change after intervention?" | "Does desflurane equipment resale value change in response to regulatory announcement?" |

### The study is novel in ALL of the following aspects:
1. **First to examine secondary market (online marketplace) prices for anaesthetic vaporizers**
2. **First to quantify the economic impact of EU F-gas regulation on medical equipment resale values**
3. **First to use eBay transaction data for anaesthesia equipment price trend analysis**
4. **First to employ concurrent agent-type controls (sevoflurane/isoflurane) to demonstrate regulation-specific price effects**
5. **First to apply time-series correlation analysis (Spearman/Kendall) to vaporizer transaction data**

## Conclusion

**The proposed study occupies a clear gap in the literature.** While extensive research exists on:
- The environmental rationale for desflurane phase-out
- The clinical implications of desflurane decommissioning  
- The drug cost economics of anaesthetic agents
- Secondary market valuation of medical equipment (general)

**No prior study has combined these domains** to examine how environmental regulation affects the secondary market value of specific anaesthetic equipment. The study is original and addresses a genuinely unexplored research question.
